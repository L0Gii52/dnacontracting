Essential Qualifications to Look For

1. Proper Licensing

Always verify contractor licenses:

  • General Contractor License (B): Required for most construction work
  • Electrical Contractor License (C-10): Required for electrical work
  • Plumbing Contractor License (C-36): Required for plumbing work
  • HVAC Contractor License (C-20): Required for heating/cooling work

Verify licenses at: www.cslb.ca.gov

2. Insurance Coverage

Ensure your contractor carries:

  • General Liability Insurance: Protects against property damage
  • Workers' Compensation: Covers worker injuries
  • Bonding: Financial protection for project completion

3. Local Experience

Choose contractors with Central Valley experience who understand:

  • Local building codes and permit processes
  • Climate considerations (heat, dust, seasonal variations)
  • Local suppliers and material availability
  • City-specific requirements in Visalia, Tulare, and Hanford

Questions to Ask Potential Contractors

Licensing and Insurance Questions

  • "Can you provide your contractor license number?"
  • "Do you have current general liability and workers' compensation insurance?"
  • "Can you provide certificates of insurance?"
  • "Are you bonded, and for what amount?"

Experience and References

  • "How long have you been in business in Central Valley?"
  • "Can you provide references from recent projects similar to mine?"
  • "Do you have experience with projects in my specific city (Visalia/Tulare/Hanford)?"
  • "Can I visit a current job site to see your work?"

Project-Specific Questions

  • "What permits will be required for this project?"
  • "How long do you estimate this project will take?"
  • "What is your payment schedule?"
  • "Do you provide a written warranty on your work?"
  • "Who will be the project manager, and how often will they be on-site?"

Red Flags to Avoid

Contractor Red Flags

  • No License or Expired License: Never work with unlicensed contractors
  • Pressure to Sign Immediately: Legitimate contractors don't pressure you
  • Cash-Only Payments: Avoid contractors who only accept cash
  • No Written Contract: Always get everything in writing
  • Unusually Low Bids: If it seems too good to be true, it probably is
  • No Local Address: Be wary of contractors without local presence
  • No References: Reputable contractors have satisfied customers
  • Asking for Full Payment Upfront: Never pay 100% before work begins

Contract Red Flags

  • Vague or incomplete project descriptions
  • No timeline or completion date
  • No warranty information
  • Unclear payment terms
  • No change order procedures

How to Get and Compare Bids

1. Get Multiple Bids

Always get at least 3 detailed bids from licensed contractors. This helps you:

  • Compare pricing and scope
  • Identify potential issues or omissions
  • Evaluate contractor communication and professionalism

2. Ensure Apples-to-Apples Comparisons

Make sure all bids include:

  • Same scope of work
  • Same materials and finishes
  • Same timeline
  • Same warranty terms
  • Same payment schedule

3. Don't Just Choose the Lowest Bid

Consider the total value, including:

  • Quality of materials
  • Timeline and reliability
  • Communication and professionalism
  • Warranty and follow-up service

Understanding Contracts and Payment

Essential Contract Elements

  • Detailed Scope of Work: Specific description of all work to be performed
  • Materials List: Brands, models, and specifications
  • Timeline: Start date, major milestones, completion date
  • Payment Schedule: When payments are due and for what work
  • Change Order Process: How changes are handled and priced
  • Warranty Information: What's covered and for how long
  • Cleanup and Disposal: Who handles debris and cleanup

Recommended Payment Schedule

  • Initial Payment: 10-20% to start work
  • Progress Payments: 25-30% at major milestones
  • Final Payment: 10-20% after completion and inspection
  • Never pay 100% upfront
